Note: Some variables such as State, Region, Locale, and Control are imported as character data. When stored as characters, summary() shows only their class and mode. After converting them to factors, summary() displays meaningful frequency counts for each category, which is the format used later when summarising categorical data.

9. Example dataset description (Task 1 write-up)

A dataset containing information on 400 higher-education institutions in the United States was analysed. The dataset includes variables describing each institution’s geographical location (state, region, and local setting), sector type (public, private, or for-profit), and whether it represents a main or branch campus. Several numeric variables provide information on undergraduate enrolment, proportions of part-time students, and costs such as in-state and out-of-state tuition. The dataset also reports the demographic composition of each institution’s student body, including the percentage of students identifying as White, Black, Hispanic, Asian, or Other.

Initial inspection shows substantial variation in enrolment and tuition levels, and a small amount of missing data (for example, 1 missing value in Locale, 24 missing values in Enrollment, PartTime, White, Black, Hispanic, Asian, and Other, and 36 missing in TuitionIn and TuitionOut). Overall, the dataset offers a clear structure suitable for exploring relationships between institutional characteristics, costs, and student demographics.
